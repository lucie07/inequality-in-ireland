About Deprivation Index

To understand why the Pobal HP Deprivation Index has become so important as a tool for developing evidence-based policies towards greater social inclusion , we have to take a brief look at how the Index is constructed.
Definition

What is Deprivation?

"The fundamental implication of the term deprivation is of an absence – of essential or desirable attributes, possessions and opportunities which are considered no more than the minimum by that society."
(Coombes et al., DoE – UK, 1995)

This definition was first advanced by Mike Coombes in 1995 as part of a review of existing deprivation indices at that time.

The 2016 Pobal HP Deprivation Index

The Pobal HP Deprivation Index is based on Small Areas, the new census geography developed jointly by the Ordnance Survey of Ireland and the Central Statistics Office for the publication of the 2011 Small Area Population Statistics.
Based on the just recently released data from the 2016 Census, the Index shows the level of overall affluence and deprivation at the level of 18,488 Small Areas in 2006, 2011 and 2016, using identical measurement scales.

About Deprivation Index

The degree of affluence and deprivation is given by the relative Pobal HP Deprivation Score, and can be displayed at various geographical levels, ranging from Small Areas, to Electoral Divisions, Local Electoral Areas, counties, or indeed any other aggregation of SAs, such as, for example, Primary Health Care Areas, Community Health Office Areas, Education Training Board Areas, Drug Task Force Areas and many more.
